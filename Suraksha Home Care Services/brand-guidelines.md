## 1. Brand Story & Personality

### 1.1 Brand Story

Suraksha Home Care Services was born from a simple observation - Indian homeowners deserve better when it comes to maintaining their homes. For too long, finding a reliable plumber, electrician, or AC technician meant relying on word-of-mouth, haggling over prices, and hoping the work would be done right.

Founded in 2024, Suraksha (meaning "protection" in Hindi, Sanskrit, and several Indian languages) set out to change this. We realized that your home is more than just a building - it is where your family grows, where memories are made, and where you should feel safe. Every leaking tap, every faulty wire, every AC that stops working on a 45-degree summer day - these are not just inconveniences. They disrupt your life.

Our founder brought together a team of experienced technicians, each trained not just in their trade but in customer service. We invested in background verification, standardized pricing, and service warranties - things that were unheard of in the Indian home services industry.

Today, Suraksha Home Care Services serves thousands of happy customers across India major cities. We have expanded from AC repair to 15+ service categories. But our core belief remains the same: every home deserves care, and every homeowner deserves peace of mind.

### 1.2 Brand Mission

"To provide every Indian home with reliable, professional, and affordable home maintenance services delivered with transparency and care."

### 1.3 Brand Vision

"To become India most trusted home care brand - synonymous with quality, reliability, and customer happiness in home services."

### 1.4 Brand Values

- **Trust (Vishwas):** Every technician is background-verified. Every service is backed by a warranty. We earn trust through consistency.
- **Transparency (Pardarshita):** No hidden charges. Upfront pricing. Clear communication. What you see is what you get.
- **Quality (Gunvatta):** Trained professionals, genuine spare parts, standardized processes. We do not cut corners.
- **Care (Dekhbhal):** We treat every home like our own. Respect for your space, time, and peace of mind is non-negotiable.
- **Reliability (Bharosa):** Same-day service. On-time arrival. We show up when we say we will.
- **Innovation (Naveenta):** Digital booking, transparent tracking, feedback-driven improvement. We use technology to serve you better.

### 1.5 Brand Personality

If Suraksha Home Care were a person, here is how we would describe them:

| Trait | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Archetype** | The Caregiver / The Guardian |
| **Gender** | Neutral (approachable, not masculine/feminine) |
| **Age** | Late 30s - mature, experienced, but energetic |
| **Appearance** | Neat, professional uniform, warm smile, tool kit in hand |
| **Voice** | Calm, reassuring, knowledgeable |
| **Communication** | Clear, simple Hindi/English, never uses jargon |
| **Attitude** | "I have got this. Relax. Your home is in safe hands." |
| **Motto** | "Aapke ghar ki suraksha, humari zimmedari." (Your home safety is our responsibility) |

### 1.6 Brand Taglines

**Primary Tagline:**
"Ghar ki suraksha, humari zimmedari." - Home safety, our responsibility.

**Secondary Taglines (for different contexts):**
- "Professional home services. Delivered with care."
- "Trust that comes home."
- "Your home deserves the best care."
- "Reliable home services, every time."
- "From AC to plumbing - we have got your home covered."

## 2. Logo Concepts

### 2.1 Logo Design Principles

- Should convey trust, protection, professionalism, and warmth
- Must work in both English and Hindi typography
- Should be recognizable at small sizes (favicon, app icon)
- Color-agnostic version needed (black/white for various backgrounds)
- Should work as a horizontal lockup (desktop) and stacked (mobile)
- Simple enough to be hand-drawn if needed
- Must stand out from competitors (Urban Company, Housejoy, etc.)

### 2.2 Logo Concept 1: "The Protective Shield"

**Description:**
A modern shield shape with a house silhouette integrated inside. The shield represents protection (Suraksha), and the house represents the home. The shield has rounded corners (friendly, not aggressive) and a subtle gradient from deep blue to teal.

**Color:**
- Shield outline: Deep Navy (#1A237E)
- House inside: Teal (#00897B)
- Background: White or transparent

**Typography:**
- "Suraksha" in bold, modern sans-serif (Montserrat Bold)
- "Home Care Services" in regular weight, smaller
- English primary, Hindi optional below

**Usage:**
- Primary logo for website, app, stationery, uniforms
- Horizontal lockup: Logo icon + text side by side
- Stacked: Logo icon above text

**Tagline Placement:**
"Ghar ki suraksha, humari zimmedari." below or beside

### 2.3 Logo Concept 2: "The Caring Hands"

**Description:**
Two hands forming a protective dome over a house. The hands are stylized, modern, and abstract - representing care and protection. The house has a chimney with a heart-shaped smoke puff, adding warmth.

**Color:**
- Hands: Deep Blue (#1565C0) outline
- House: Orange (#FF6F00) for warmth
- Heart: Warm Orange (#FF8F00)

**Typography:**
- Handwritten-style font for "Suraksha" (warm, personal feel)
- Clean sans-serif for "Home Care Services"

**Usage:**
- Brand applications needing warmth and emotional connection
- Marketing materials, customer communications
- Social media profile pictures

### 2.4 Logo Concept 3: "The Modern Monogram"

**Description:**
A minimalist monogram using "S" and "H" (Suraksha Home) intertwined. The S forms a subtle shield shape around the H. Extremely modern, minimal, and scalable. Works beautifully as an app icon and favicon.

**Color:**
- Single color: Deep Teal (#00695C)
- Can be used in brand colors

**Typography:**
- Company full name spelled out in clean sans-serif next to monogram
- Monogram is the hero element

**Usage:**
- App icon
- Favicon
- Watermark on images
- Small format applications
- Merchandise (caps, t-shirts, mugs)

### 2.5 Logo Concept 4: "The Tool and Home"

**Description:**
A creative combination of a wrench and a house. The wrench handle forms the base of the house, and the house roof incorporates a screwdriver shape. It directly communicates "home services/tools" without being too literal.

**Color:**
- Wrench/House: Gradient from Navy (#1A237E) to Teal (#00897B)
- Accent: Warm Orange (#FF6F00) for the roof/screwdriver highlight

**Typography:**
- Bold industrial font for "Suraksha" (Oswald or similar)
- Clean contrast with "Home Care Services" in light weight

**Usage:**
- Service vehicles
- Tool kits and equipment
- Workwear (small logo on chest/back)
- Heavy-duty applications (banners, signage)

### 2.6 Logo Concept 5: "The Indian Home"

**Description:**
A traditional Indian-style house (with a slight dome/arch reminiscent of Indian architecture) enclosed in a circular boundary. The circle represents completeness and protection. Inside the house, subtle Indian motifs (minimal). The design nods to Indian heritage while remaining modern.

**Color:**
- Circle: Saffron Orange (#FF6F00) + Deep Blue (#1565C0) ring
- House: White with Deep Blue details
- Background: Transparent or Deep Blue

**Typography:**
- Bilingual: English + Hindi (Devanagari script) both prominent
- "Suraksha" in Hindi above English
- Script: Modern Devanagari font + English sans-serif

**Usage:**
- Primary brand logo for India-focused marketing
- Packaging, local advertising
- Community engagement materials
- Bilingual communications

### 2.7 Logo Usage Rules

**Clear Space:**
- Minimum clear space around logo = Height of "Suraksha" text
- No text, graphics, or other elements within this space

**Minimum Sizes:**
- Horizontal lockup: 150px wide (digital), 1.5 inches (print)
- Stacked lockup: 100px wide (digital), 1 inch (print)
- Icon only (favicon): 32x32px minimum

**Prohibited Uses:**
- Do not stretch or distort the logo
- Do not change logo colors beyond approved palette
- Do not add drop shadows or effects
- Do not place on busy backgrounds without sufficient contrast
- Do not rotate the logo
- Do not rearrange elements
- Do not use partial logo (use icon-only version instead)
- Do not outline or add borders

**Background Variations:**

| Background Color | Logo Version |
|-----------------|--------------|
| White / Light | Full color logo |
| Brand Blue | White logo |
| Dark / Black | White logo |
| Photo (light area) | Full color logo |
| Photo (dark area) | White logo |
| Any brand color (dark) | White logo |
| Any brand color (light) | Full color logo |

### 3.4 Color Usage Guidelines

**Hierarchy:**
- Primary Deep Navy should dominate (60-70% of brand applications)
- Trust Teal should be the primary accent (20-25%)
- Saffron Orange used sparingly for energy (5-10%)
- Other colors as needed but sparingly

**Accessibility:**
- All text on colored backgrounds must meet WCAG AA contrast ratio (4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text)
- Deep Navy on White: Passes AAA
- White on Deep Navy: Passes AAA
- Saffron Orange on White: Passes AA (large text only)
- Avoid light text on light backgrounds and dark text on dark backgrounds

**Gradients:**
- Primary gradient: Deep Navy (#1A237E) to Trust Teal (#00897B)
- Warm gradient: Saffron Orange (#FF6F00) to Warm Orange (#FF8F00)
- Use gradients sparingly - prefer solid colors for most applications

## 4. Typography

### 4.1 Primary Font (Headings and Display)

**Font: Montserrat**

| Weight | Usage | Size Range |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Montserrat Bold (700) | Primary headings (H1, H2) | 24px - 48px |
| Montserrat SemiBold (600) | Subheadings (H3, H4) | 18px - 30px |
| Montserrat Medium (500) | Section headers, nav items | 14px - 20px |
| Montserrat Regular (400) | Large text, pull quotes | 16px - 24px |

**Why Montserrat:**
- Clean, modern, highly readable
- Excellent legibility at all sizes
- Geometric design conveys professionalism
- Wide range of weights available (free on Google Fonts)
- Works well in both English and Hindi (with appropriate fallbacks)

### 4.2 Secondary Font (Body Text)

**Font: Open Sans**

| Weight | Usage | Size Range |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Open Sans Regular (400) | Body paragraphs, descriptions | 14px - 18px |
| Open Sans SemiBold (600) | Emphasis, strong text | 14px - 18px |
| Open Sans Light (300) | Captions, small text, footnotes | 11px - 13px |

**Why Open Sans:**
- Excellent readability for extended text
- Neutral, friendly appearance
- Pairs well with Montserrat (different x-height creates contrast)
- Also available on Google Fonts
- Optimized for both print and digital

### 4.3 Hindi / Devanagari Font

**Font: Noto Sans Devanagari**

| Weight | Usage | Size Range |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Noto Sans Devanagari Regular | Hindi body text | 14px - 18px |
| Noto Sans Devanagari Bold | Hindi headings | 18px - 36px |

### 4.4 Web Font Stack

English Headings: Montserrat, Segoe UI, Arial, sans-serif
English Body: Open Sans, Segoe UI, Arial, sans-serif
Hindi: Noto Sans Devanagari, Nirmala UI, Mangal, sans-serif

### 4.5 Typography Scale

| Level | Desktop | Mobile | Weight | Font |
|-------|---------|--------|--------|------|
| H1 | 40px / 48px line-height | 28px / 36px line-height | Bold (700) | Montserrat |

| Nav | 14px / 16px line-height | 16px / 20px line-height | Medium (500) | Montserrat |

### 4.6 Typography Rules

- Maximum line length: 70-80 characters (web), 50-60 characters (print)
- Heading-to-body ratio: Minimum 1:1.5
- Do not use all caps except for short labels or acronyms (max 5 words)
- Avoid underlining (use bold or color for emphasis)
- Hyphenation: Do not hyphenate headings; hyphenate body text only if needed
- Alignment: Left-align for Indian languages, center only for short headings or callouts
- Do not use more than 3 font sizes per page/section
- Do not use more than 2 font families on any given page

## 5. Brand Voice and Tone

### 5.1 Brand Voice Characteristics

| Characteristic | Style | Example |
|---------------|-------|---------|
| **Warm** | Friendly, human, never robotic | "Your AC is fixed and ready to cool your home!" |
| **Reassuring** | Calm, confident, trustworthy | "Do not worry. Our technician will be there in 30 minutes." |
| **Knowledgeable** | Expert but not arrogant | "Based on our experience, servicing your AC twice a year extends its life by 3-5 years." |
| **Simple** | Clear, no jargon, easy to understand | "We check the gas, clean the filter, and make sure everything is working." |
| **Respectful** | Polite, formal but not stiff | "Thank you for choosing Suraksha Home Care. We appreciate your trust." |
| **Proactive** | Helpful, anticipates needs | "Your AC was last serviced 6 months ago. Ready for another check-up?" |

### 5.2 Voice Dos and Donts

| Do | Dont |
|----|------|
| Use simple English (or Hinglish when appropriate) | Use technical jargon without explanation |
| Be warm and friendly | Be overly casual or slangy |
| Use "we" and "you" | Use third person ("the company", "the customer") |
| Be specific ("Your AC will be serviced within 2 hours") | Be vague ("We will get to it soon") |
| Acknowledge emotions ("We understand it is frustrating") | Be dismissive ("Just relax, it is not a big deal") |
| Offer solutions proactively | Just say sorry without offering a fix |
| Use Indian cultural references appropriately | Use cultural references that might exclude or offend |

### 5.3 Tone by Channel

| Channel | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Website | Professional, warm, informative | "Professional home services you can trust. Book online in 2 minutes." |
| Social Media | Friendly, engaging, casual | "Monday morning, and your AC is already acting up? We get it. Book a same-day service!" |
| WhatsApp | Personal, direct, helpful | "Hi Rahul! Your service is confirmed for tomorrow at 10 AM. Our technician Amit will be there." |
| Email | Professional, personal, value-driven | "We hope this email finds you well. Here is a guide to preparing your home for monsoon season." |
| SMS | Short, clear, actionable | "Reminder: AC service tomorrow 10 AM. Reply C to confirm. - Suraksha Home Care" |
| Print/Flyer | Clear, benefit-focused, local | "AC Service starting at just Rs 499! Call now: XXXXXXXXXX" |
| Phone | Warm, patient, helpful | "Namaste! This is Suraksha Home Care. How can we help you today?" |

### 5.4 Common Phrases and Expressions

**Greetings:**
- "Namaste! Welcome to Suraksha Home Care."
- "Hello! How can we help with your home today?"

**Booking Confirmation:**
- "Your booking is confirmed! Our technician will arrive on [date] at [time]."
- "Service booked! Here is what to expect: [steps]"

**Service Reminder:**
- "Friendly reminder: Your service is scheduled for tomorrow."
- "Our technician [name] will be visiting your home tomorrow at [time]."

**Post-Service:**
- "Your [service] is complete! Here is how to keep it in top condition:"
- "Thank you for trusting us with your home. We hope you loved the service!"

**Follow-up:**
- "It has been [X] months since your last service. Time for a check-up?"
- "We value your feedback. How was your experience with [technician name]?"

**Apology / Service Recovery:**
- "We sincerely apologize for the inconvenience. Here is what we will do to make it right."
- "We are sorry the service did not meet your expectations. We will send a senior technician tomorrow at no extra charge."

**Seasonal:**
- Summer: "Summer is here! Is your AC ready? Book a service today."
- Monsoon: "Monsoon is knocking. Protect your home with our waterproofing services."
- Winter: "Winter is coming. Ensure your geyser is working perfectly."
- Festive: "Diwali is almost here! Let us help make your home sparkle."

## 6. Imagery Style Guide

### 6.1 Photography Style

**What Our Photos Should Look Like:**
- Bright, well-lit images (natural light preferred)
- Clean, organized scenes (before/after style)
- Real technicians, not models
- Actual service in progress (not staged)
- Warm color temperature (slightly warm, not cool/clinical)
- Candid moments preferred over posed
- Indian homes and settings (relatable to target audience)

**Photo Categories:**

| Category | Style | Example Subjects |
|----------|-------|-----------------|
| Service in Progress | Action shot, clean background, technician in uniform | AC cleaning process, plumbing repair, deep cleaning |
| Before/After | Side-by-side or swipe comparison, same lighting | Dirty AC filter vs clean, clogged sink vs clear |
| Team and People | Warm, smiling, approachable | Technician portrait, team group photo, customer interaction |
| Tools and Equipment | Clean product shot style | Tool kit, spare parts, equipment |
| Results | Focus on quality, detail shots | Sparkling kitchen, clean AC vents, painted wall |
| Behind the Scenes | Authentic, not staged | Training session, team huddle, van loading |
| Customer Stories | Real homes, real people (with permission) | Family in clean living room, satisfied customer |

### 6.2 Photography Dos and Donts

| Do | Dont |
|----|------|
| Use real technicians in uniform | Use stock photos of models in hard hats |
| Show actual Indian homes and settings | Show western-style homes or interiors |
| Capture natural emotions (smile, relief, satisfaction) | Use forced or fake expressions |
| Keep backgrounds clean and professional | Show messy, cluttered, or unprofessional settings |
| Use warm, natural lighting | Use harsh flash or cold fluorescent light |
| Show the process AND the result | Only show the result (misses the value story) |
| Get signed releases for all identifiable people | Use customer photos without permission |

### 6.3 Video Style

**Tone:**
- Educational, helpful, warm
- Not overly produced (authentic over polished)
- Fast-paced for social (Reels/Shorts), moderate pace for YouTube

**Technical Specs:**
- Resolution: 1080p minimum (4K preferred for YouTube)
- Aspect Ratio: 16:9 (YouTube), 9:16 (Reels/Shorts/Stories), 1:1 (Facebook/Instagram feed)
- Lighting: Bright, even lighting on the subject
- Audio: Clear voiceover or on-camera audio (lapel mic recommended)
- Color grade: Warm, slightly boosted contrast, natural skin tones
- Duration: 15-30 seconds (Shorts/Reels), 5-15 minutes (YouTube)
- Branding: Intro (3 seconds logo), outro (subscribe/CTA), lower thirds for names

### 6.4 Illustration Style

- Flat vector illustrations with rounded corners
- Color palette: Navy, Teal, White, with Saffron Orange accents
- Style: Modern, friendly, approachable
- Use for: Infographics, icons, process illustrations, blog feature images
- Avoid: Overly complex illustrations, photo-realistic vector art, clipart

### 6.5 Iconography

- Style: Line icons with rounded stroke ends (2px stroke weight)
- Color: Trust Teal (#00897B) for active, Medium Gray (#9E9E9E) for inactive
- Size: 24x24px (standard), 32x32px (large), 48x48px (hero)
- Library: Use Feather Icons or Phosphor Icons as base (customize to brand colors)
- Icon set must include: AC unit, wrench, tap, lightbulb, broom, spray, paint roller, screwdriver, house, phone, calendar, clock, shield, star, checkmark, truck
