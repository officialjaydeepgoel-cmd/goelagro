India's home services market is projected to grow from **.5 billion in 2024 to .8 billion by 2030**, at a CAGR of 17.4%. Suraksha Home Care Services is positioned to capture a significant share of this growing market by offering standardized, high-quality, and trustworthy home cleaning services.

## 1.2 Market Analysis
### Industry Overview
The Indian home cleaning services market is driven by rising urbanization, dual-income households, increased health awareness post-COVID, and a growing willingness to outsource household chores.
**Key Market Drivers:**
- Urbanization Rate: 35% (projected 43% by 2030)
- Dual-Income Households: Growing at 12% YoY
- Health Consciousness: 73% of urban Indians now prioritize home sanitization
- Online Service Discovery: 68% of customers find home services via internet search

# 4. Brand Story
## 4.1 The Founding Narrative
It started with a single observation: India's middle class was growing, apartments were getting smaller, time was getting shorter, and yet -- the one place that was supposed to be our safest haven -- our homes -- was becoming harder to maintain.
**Rahul Mehra, our founder**, experienced this firsthand. Working 12-hour days in Bangalore's tech hub, he came home to a dusty 2BHK that never felt truly clean. He tried booking cleaners online -- the first one never showed up, the second damaged his sofa, and the third quoted double the price midway through the job.
"The home cleaning industry in India is broken," Rahul realized. "No standards, no accountability, no trust."
He started Suraksha Home Care Services in 2024 with a simple belief: **cleaning is not a luxury, it is a necessity -- and every family deserves a reliable way to get it done.**
What began as a one-man operation with a bucket, a mop, and sheer determination has grown into a movement. Suraksha means "safety" in Sanskrit -- and safety is exactly what we bring. Safety from germs, safety from unreliable service, safety from the stress of managing home maintenance.
## 4.2 Brand Promise
> *"When Suraksha knocks on your door, you don't just get a cleaner home. You get peace of mind. Trained professionals. Eco-friendly products. And a 100% satisfaction guarantee -- or we redo it for free."*
# 5. Unique Selling Proposition (USP)
## 10 Key Differentiators
### 1. Certified and Trained Professionals
Every Suraksha technician completes a 7-day mandatory training program covering cleaning techniques, chemical handling, customer etiquette, and safety protocols. We do not hire day laborers -- we employ career cleaners.
### 2. 50-Point Quality Checklist
Every job, big or small, is verified against our comprehensive 50-point checklist. Supervisors conduct random inspections to ensure standards are maintained.
### 3. Eco-Friendly and Hypoallergenic Products
We use only EPA-approved, biodegradable cleaning chemicals that are safe for children, pets, and the elderly. No harsh ammonia, no toxic fumes.
### 4. 100% Satisfaction Guarantee
If you are not happy with any part of our service, we will redo it within 48 hours at no additional cost. No questions asked.
### 5. Real-Time Booking and Tracking
Book via app, website, or WhatsApp. Track your technician in real-time. Get photo evidence of completed work. Digital receipts and invoices.
### 6. Insurance-Backed Services
All Suraksha employees are verified, background-checked, and insured. Any accidental damage during service is covered up to Rs 50,000.
### 7. Transparent Pricing -- No Surprises
Price quoted is the price you pay. No mid-job price hikes, no hidden charges. We publish standard rate cards on our website.
### 8. Subscription Plans for Regular Customers
Save up to 25% with weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly subscription plans. Auto-scheduled, auto-billed, zero hassle.
### 9. Specialized Equipment
Industrial-grade HEPA vacuum cleaners, steam cleaners, pressure washers -- equipment you won't find with local cleaners.
### 10. Technology-First Experience
SMS confirmations, WhatsApp reminders, digital invoices, online payments, customer dashboard, and loyalty rewards -- all integrated into a seamless tech platform.

# 28. Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)
## 28.1 SOP for Full Home Deep Cleaning
### Preparation Phase (15 minutes)
1. **Arrival Protocol**
   - Arrive 5-10 minutes before scheduled time
   - Wear clean uniform with ID card visible
   - Call customer upon arrival: "Hello, this is [Name] from Suraksha. I've arrived at your building."
   - Wait at door, do not enter until invited
2. **Pre-Service Walkthrough**
   - Request customer to walk through all areas with you
   - Note any fragile items, high-priority areas, special instructions
   - Identify any stains or damage that existed before service (document with photo)
   - Confirm scope of work matches booking
3. **Setup**
   - Lay drop cloths in high-traffic areas
   - Set up equipment (vacuum, mop, chemical caddy)
   - Fill buckets with water (two-bucket method: one cleaning, one rinsing)
   - Prepare chemical dilutions as per ratios
   - Take pre-service photos of all rooms
### Execution Phase
4. **Top-to-Bottom, Left-to-Right Approach**
   - Start with ceiling corners, walls, fixtures, surfaces, floors
   - Process each room in this order:
     a. Remove loose items (decor, cushions) and set aside
     b. Dry dust all surfaces from top to bottom
     c. Vacuum carpets/rugs
     d. Clean windows and mirrors
     e. Wipe all surfaces with appropriate cleaner
     f. Spot clean walls if needed
     g. Vacuum/mop floors last
5. **Room Sequence (Optimal)**
   - Bedrooms first (least soiled, cleaner tools)
   - Living room
   - Kitchen (requires separate cloths and water)
   - Bathrooms last (most soiled, use dedicated cloths)
   - Balcony/utility area
6. **Chemical Usage Protocol**
   - All-purpose cleaner: For general surfaces, countertops
   - Glass cleaner: Mirrors, windows, glass tables
   - Degreaser: Kitchen surfaces, chimney, stove
   - Bathroom cleaner: Tiles, toilet, sink stains
   - Disinfectant: High-touch points, door handles, switches
   - Floor cleaner: Appropriate for floor type (wood, tile, marble)
### Completion Phase (15 minutes)
7. **Final Inspection**
   - Walk through all serviced areas with supervisor
   - Check against 50-point checklist
   - Ensure no streaks, residue, or missed spots
   - Take post-service photos (same angles as pre-service)
8. **Customer Walkthrough**
   - Invite customer to inspect the work
   - Point out any areas that required extra attention
   - Answer any questions
   - Request digital sign-off on employee app
9. **Cleanup and Departure**
   - Pack all equipment, ensure nothing left behind
   - Dispose of waste in customer's trash bin (with permission)
   - Remove shoe covers, sanitize hands
   - Thank the customer: "Thank you for choosing Suraksha. We hope you love your clean home!"
## 28.2 SOP for Kitchen Deep Cleaning
### Specific Steps
1. **Empty Preparation**
   - Clear countertops completely
   - Request customer to remove perishables from refrigerator (if inside clean requested)
   - Remove stove grates and burner caps
2. **Chimney Cleaning**
   - Switch off and unplug chimney
   - Remove mesh filters and soak in degreasing solution (15 mins)
   - Wipe chimney body and motor casing with degreaser
   - Scrub filters with soft brush, rinse, dry completely
   - Reassemble and check operation
3. **Stove/Hob Cleaning**
   - Remove grates and burners
   - Soak removable parts in warm soapy water
   - Apply degreaser to hob surface, let sit for 5 mins
   - Scrub with non-abrasive pad
   - Wipe clean, dry, reassemble
4. **Counter and Backsplash**
   - Apply degreaser, let sit for 2-3 mins
   - Scrub with appropriate pad
   - Wipe with damp microfiber cloth
   - Sanitize with food-safe disinfectant
5. **Cabinets**
   - Exterior: Degrease, wipe, polish (wood-specific product)
   - Interior (if included): Empty shelves, vacuum crumbs, wipe with mild cleaner
6. **Sink**
   - Sprinkle baking soda, scrub with sponge
   - Rinse with vinegar solution (deodorize)
   - Polish faucet with microfiber cloth
7. **Floor**
   - Sweep thoroughly
   - Mop with degreasing floor cleaner
   - Pay special attention to corners and under appliances
## 28.3 SOP for Sofa/Upholstery Cleaning
1. **Inspection**
   - Identify fabric type (check care tag if visible)
   - Test cleaning solution on inconspicuous area (15-second colorfast test)
   - Document any pre-existing stains with photos
2. **Pre-Vacuum**
   - Remove all cushions
   - Vacuum entire sofa including crevices, under cushions, back panels
   - Use crevice tool for tight spaces
3. **Spot Treatment**
   - Identify stain type (food, ink, oil, pet)
   - Apply appropriate spot treatment
   - Blot (never rub) with clean microfiber cloth
   - Allow to sit for 5-10 minutes
4. **Shampooing (Machine)**
   - Prepare cleaning solution as per manufacturer instructions
   - Fill hot water extraction machine
   - Work in sections (2x2 ft areas)
   - Apply solution, agitate with brush, extract with machine
   - Overlap sections by 2 inches to avoid streaks
5. **Drying**
   - Open windows, turn on fans/dehumidifier
   - Place aluminum foil under furniture legs (prevent rust transfer)
   - Advise customer: "Please avoid sitting for 4-6 hours until completely dry"
   - Leave room with adequate ventilation

## 31.2 Chemical Handling Protocols
### DOs
- DO read labels before every use
- DO dilute chemicals exactly as per instructions
- DO wear appropriate PPE when mixing chemicals
- DO label all spray bottles clearly (content + dilution date)
- DO store chemicals in original containers with labels intact
- DO wash hands after handling any chemical
- DO keep all chemicals away from food and eating areas
### DON'Ts
- NEVER mix bleach with ammonia (produces toxic gas)
- NEVER mix bleach with acidic cleaners (produces chlorine gas)
- NEVER use chemical containers for drinking water
- NEVER leave chemicals unattended in a customer's home
- NEVER spray chemicals near open flames or electrical outlets
- NEVER use expired chemicals (check monthly rotation)
- NEVER transfer chemicals to unlabeled containers
### Emergency Chemical Response
| Incident | Immediate Action | Follow-up |
|----------|-----------------|-----------|
| Skin contact | Flush with water 15 mins, remove contaminated clothing | Report to supervisor, seek medical if irritation persists |
| Eye contact | Flush eyes with clean water 15 mins (eyewash station) | Immediate medical attention |
| Inhalation | Move to fresh air, sit upright | Seek medical if difficulty breathing |
| Ingestion | Do NOT induce vomiting, rinse mouth | Call Poison Control (104) immediately |
| Spill | Contain with absorbent material, ventilate area | Clean with appropriate neutralizer |
## 31.3 Workplace Safety
### Electrical Safety
- Check cords for fraying before use
- Never use electrical equipment near water
- Keep cords out of walkways (tape down)
- Use GFCI-protected outlets for all equipment
- Unplug equipment before cleaning or servicing
### Ladder Safety
- Inspect ladder before each use (cracks, loose screws)
- Maintain 3-point contact when climbing
- Never stand on top 2 rungs
- Place on stable, level surface
- Never overreach -- move the ladder instead
- Maximum height: 4 ft for step ladder without scaffolding
### Fire Safety
- Know the location of fire extinguisher at every job site
- Identify two exits in every home/office
- No smoking on the job
- Store flammable chemicals away from heat sources
- Report any gas leaks immediately to customer and vacate
## 31.4 Emergency Response Plan
### Medical Emergency
1. **Assess** -- Is the person conscious? Breathing? Bleeding?
2. **Call** -- Dial 108 (Emergency) or local ambulance
3. **First Aid** -- Apply basic first aid until help arrives
4. **Notify** -- Call supervisor immediately
5. **Document** -- Complete incident report within 24 hours
### Fire Emergency
1. **Alert** -- Shout "FIRE!" and alert everyone
2. **Evacuate** -- Leave immediately, use stairs not elevator
3. **Call** -- Dial 101 (Fire Department)
4. **Extinguish** -- Only if fire is small and you have clear exit
5. **Account** -- Headcount at assembly point
### Workplace Injury
1. **Stop work** immediately
2. **First aid** for minor injuries
3. **Medical attention** for serious injuries
4. **Report** to supervisor within 1 hour
5. **Document** -- Incident report within 24 hours
6. **Investigate** -- Root cause analysis within 48 hours
